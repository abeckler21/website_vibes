# research

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning interpretability, visual analytics, and human–computer interaction. I work with Gordon Kindlmann at the University of Chicago on Non-Equivariance Revealed on Orbits (NERO), a visualization system for analyzing how neural networks respond to transformations in input data. I also collaborate with Dennis Shasha at NYU on PatentNerd, an AI-powered platform that uses large language models to evaluate and refine patent claims. Together, these projects combine computational rigor with visual reasoning — helping humans better understand how models and ideas behave.

# teaching

I've served as a Teaching Assistant for several core computer science courses at the University of Chicago, including Introduction to Computer Science I & II and Data Visualization. I also was one of the primary instructors for the 2023 Summer Coding at UChicago: Builder's Academy program here at the UChicago, teaching programming fundamentals to 25 Chicago public school students over the course of a summer. One more: I founded and led a Girls Who Code program right here at Carnegie Elementary School that introduced middle-school students to creative coding, robotics, and computational thinking — experiences that shaped my belief in accessible, inclusive CS education.
